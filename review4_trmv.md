We evaluate the geometry of our generated 3D worlds by comparing the predicted depth maps in SphericalDreamer with other 360$^\circ$ depth estimation models. 

**Qualitatively,** ([anon link](https://anon-supp.github.io/), section D), we observe that the depth maps computed in SphericalDreamer are the most accurate and present the least artifacts compared to other approaches.

### Ablation study

We conduct a comprehensive ablation study to evaluate the contributions of two key components of our pipeline: Harmonic Blending and Layered Depth Images (LDI). Specifically, we (i) remove LDI from the pipeline, and (ii) replace Harmonic Blending with simpler alternatives—naïve blending, bilinear interpolation, and diffusion-based depth inpainting (InFusion [5]).

**Qualitatively** ([anon link](https://anon-supp.github.io/), Section B), both ablations lead to noticeable degradation. Replacing Harmonic Blending results in geometry artefacts in transition regions, whereas removing LDI introduces prominent disocclusion artifacts, with missing background content appearing as unfilled regions. In contrast, the full model produces complete and coherent environments.

**Quantitatively,** we evaluate performance using multiple complementary metrics:
- C-CLIP for cross-view semantic consistency,
- CLIP-Score for text alignment,
- BRISQUE, CLIP-IQA, and Q-Align for perceptual rendering quality.

As shown in the table below, both ablations lead to consistent drops across all metrics, highlighting the importance of each component. In particular, Harmonic Blending improves completeness and visual quality, while LDI is critical for handling occlusions and maintaining geometric and cross-view consistency.

| Setting | BRISQUE (↓) | Coverage (↑) | CLIP-Score (↑) | C-CLIP (↑) | CLIP-IQA (↑) | Q-Align (↑) |
|:-------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| no LDI | 45.8937 | 0.9652 | 0.3371 | 0.8459 | 0.7358 | 2.1935 |
| no HB (naive instead) | 47.1084 | 0.9242 | 0.3253 | 0.8001 | 0.6503 | 1.9953 |
| no HB (inpainting instead) | *44.3742* | 0.9314 | 0.3272 | 0.8099 | 0.5976 | 2.078 |
| no HB (interpolation instead) | 45.4247 | *0.9835* | *0.3377* | *0.8496* | *0.7505* | *2.2595* |
| Ours | **43.4709** | **0.9993** | **0.3418** | **0.8608** | **0.7582** | **2.366** |

In summary, both components are essential: Harmonic Blending ensures coherent geometry in transition regions, and LDI enables accurate modeling of occluded scene content, jointly contributing to high-quality and consistent 3D world generation.

#### Comparisons of Harmonic blending with simpler alternatives

We compare harmonic blending to alternative depth completion methods by masking regions of a reference depth map and reconstructing them.

**Qualitatively** ([anon link](https://anon-supp.github.io/), Section B), harmonic blending produces the smoothest transitions between known and reconstructed regions, without introducing artifacts.

**Quantitatively**, it achieves the best Depth Transition Score, which measures the average depth discontinuity across the boundary between known and reconstructed regions (lower is smoother), and the lowest Depth Estimation Error, which evaluates the deviation from ground-truth depth near the blending boundary (lower indicates more accurate and coherent geometry). These results confirm that our blending strategy ensures seamless alignment in overlapping areas.

## Dealing with highly non-linear camera trajectories

Harmonic blending is formulated in a general way and is not restricted to linear trajectories. For instance, Figure 8 of the paper illustrates its application to highly non-linear trajectories. However, for simplicity and to enable direct comparison with prior work, we focus on linear trajectories in our experiments.
